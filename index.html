The Science of Smiles

Triple-action formula backed by research and trusted by dentists

Xylitol: The Cavity Fighter

1. Starving the "Bad" Bacteria

The primary cause of cavities is the bacteria Streptococcus mutans. Unlike regular sugar, these bacteria cannot digest Xylitol. When they consume Xylitol, their energy-producing process is neutralized and they eventually die off, significantly reducing the bacterial load in a child's mouth.

2. Reducing Plaque Formation

Xylitol makes it harder for bacteria to stick to the surfaces of teeth. By reducing the "stickiness" of plaque, it becomes much easier for a child to brush away buildup, even with their developing brushing skills.

3. Encouraging Remineralization

Xylitol helps maintain a neutral pH level in the mouth and increases saliva flow. Saliva is rich in minerals like calcium and phosphate; by keeping the mouth at a healthy pH, Xylitol helps the saliva deposit these minerals back into the tooth enamel, repairing early-stage microscopic cavities.

4. Long-Term Dental Protection

Studies show that consistent use of Xylitol-based products in early childhood can lead to a lasting reduction in tooth decay, even years after the child stops using that specific product.

Sodium Monofluorophosphate: The Enamel Shield

While Sorbitol and Xylitol manage the oral environment and bacteria, Sodium Monofluorophosphate acts directly on the tooth structure itself. It is a specialized form of fluoride preferred in gel formulations for its stability and gentle delivery.

1. Strengthening Developing Enamel

Children's teeth are still "maturing" even after they erupt. SMFP works through a process called Remineralization — it integrates into the tooth's crystal lattice, converting hydroxyapatite into Fluorapatite, which is significantly harder and more resistant to acid attacks.

2. Superior Acid Resistance

Every time a child eats, the mouth's pH drops. SMFP provides a protective layer that makes the enamel less soluble in acidic conditions, so even after fruit juices or snacks, the "Nagumo Shield" helps prevent breakdown of the tooth surface.

3. Safe and Controlled Delivery

The fluoride is released gradually and at levels precisely calibrated for children, ensuring maximum protective benefit without overexposure — giving parents complete peace of mind.

4. Stability in Gel Formulations

SMFP is highly compatible with the silica-based abrasives used in modern tooth gels. This ensures that the fluoride remains active and effective throughout the entire shelf life of the product.

Sorbitol: The Natural Sweetener

1. Anti-Cavity Sweetener

A naturally occurring sugar alcohol that bacteria cannot metabolize into acid, preventing tooth decay while still delivering a pleasant sweetness that children enjoy.

2. Texture & Moisture Retention

Acts as a humectant, keeping the gel moist and smooth for better application and a more comfortable brushing experience every morning and night.

3. Soothing Cooling Effect

Provides a pleasant cooling sensation during brushing that kids love, making the routine something to look forward to rather than resist.
